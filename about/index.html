Since 2007 I've been in the GIS field professionally and for the past nine plus years I have been working as a remote GIS Consultant supporting an array of clientele. Having gained exposure to GIS while at Penn State University and interning with the National Park Service, I landed a typical entry level GIS role with an environmental evaluation company. After demonstrating my capabilities I earned the opportunity to fill their GIS Specialist position when it became vacant where I supported the company's Aerial Imagery, Fire Insurance, Geocoding and Topo Departments. In 2013 they were acquired by a larger company setting up the perfect opportunity for me to branch out on my own where I'd be able to consult both on-site as well as remotely.

Which brings us to today where I continue serving as a seamless extension to GIS departments needing just that extra little bit of help and/or guidance. At this point in my career there isn't much that I can't problem solve or reverse engineer and the people I've worked with are at ease tossing wide ranging inquiries my direction assured that I'll come through whether it's specifically GIS related or not. I'm equally adept dealing with raster, vector, mesh and point cloud input of all magnitudes that's either been provided or that I've had to retrieve myself. Sometimes that means scraping and batch processing hundreds of image tiles using a customized script, retrieving and reformatting geojson data from a form for other analysts to use or grabbing elements from an interactive map retaining pertinent attributes.
